Estimation of demand with decreasing block pricing and market-level data

Discrete-continuous choice (DCC) models are commonly used to estimate demand for products with decreasing or increasing block rates. Using the pure characteristics model of Berry and Pakes (2007), I propose a method to estimate a structural model of discrete-continuous choice using aggregate market share data and observed consumption block characteristics. I apply this method to the estimation of demand for natural gas in Quebec.

Presented at: Competition Bureau of Canada (Invited Seminar, 2025) · Congrès SCSE (2025) · CIREQ Econometric Conference (Poster, 2025) · CESG Annual Meeting (Poster, 2024)

Demand for prepaid mobile phone in Burkina Faso

The prepaid pay-as-you-go plan is widespread across countries — amounting to 71% of active plans globally and 90% in Africa. This paper studies whether joint consumption arises from complementarity between operators or correlated preferences, and examines synergies of services across operators, using survey data from Burkina Faso.

Presented at: Competition Bureau of Canada (Invited Seminar, 2025) · Association des femmes scientifiques africaines (2025)
