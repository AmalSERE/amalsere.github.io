Demand for prepaid mobile phone in Burkina Faso

Job Market Paper

The prepaid pay-as-you-go plan is widespread across countries — amounting to 71% of active plans globally and 90% in Africa. The literature highlights that contracts flexibility, enforcement of termination tariffs, and limited service reliability contribute to the high prevalence of multi-SIM consumers in low-income countries. This paper contributes to reducing this gap by studying the Burkinabe case using data from a recent survey, distinguishing whether joint consumption arises from complementarity between operators or correlated preferences, and examining synergies of services across operators.

Presented at: Competition Bureau of Canada (Invited Seminar, 2025) · Association des femmes scientifiques africaines (2025)

Estimation of demand with decreasing block pricing and market-level data

Discrete-continuous choice (DCC) models are commonly used to estimate demand for products with decreasing or increasing block rates. Standard estimation methods for DCC models require individual-level consumption. Using the pure characteristics model of Berry and Pakes (2007), I propose a method to estimate a structural model of discrete-continuous choice using aggregate market share data and observed consumption block characteristics. I apply this method to the estimation of demand for natural gas in Quebec. The institutional background is used to introduce new instruments in this literature. The conditional and unconditional price elasticities are close to previous estimates.

Presented at: Competition Bureau of Canada (Invited Seminar, 2025) · Congrès SCSE (2025) · CIREQ Econometric Conference (Poster, 2025) · CESG Annual Meeting (Poster, 2024)
